Improved housing in Africa

2025—2025 •

The prevalence of improved housing in an area can offer security, shelter from weather events, and protection from disease exposure. The United Nations defines an imrpoved house as having sufficient space (not overcrowded), sanitation facilities, water supply, construction using improved materials, and security of tenure.

Our geospatial analysis quantified the prevalence of the first four of these quantities (security of tenure is hard to model due to lack of data) and, subsequently, the prevalence of improved housing in Africa. These modelled outputs will inform, among other things, the risk of malaria in Sub-Saharan Africa.

The target data for this modelling effort largely came from DHS and MICS surveys. We informed our models using a range of socio-economic indicators (e.g., policy indicators, GDP, accessibility to cities) as well as sattelite observations (e.g., night-time lights, built environments).

## Why write about traffic?
Most people where I live use cars to get around. Peak hour traffic or incidental traffic has been unavoidable recently. With limited means for distraction, traffic gives us too much time to think. Why are we moving so slowly? Am I in the optimal lane? Did I choose the right route? Who is to blame for my current misery? My natural inclination is to use mathematics to try and answer these questions. And of course I'm not the first person with a background in maths to do the same. I wanted an excuse to dive a little bit deeper, and making a series of blog posts seems like a good enough reason. 

## Some more background
This is not my first time viewing traffic through a mathematical lens. In my third year mathematical methods class we used a first order hyperbolic partial differential equation to interrogate traffic dynamics. By applying simple conservation rules, we discover non-unique solutions for the traffic speed and shock behaviour. Such models clearly inspired me, as this was the period in which I was fully converted to an applied mathematician.
